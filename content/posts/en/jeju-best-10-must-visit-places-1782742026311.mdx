# The 10 Best Places to Visit in Jeju: A Local's Guide to Korea's Island Paradise

As summer travel season kicks into full swing, more and more travelers are setting their sights on Jeju Island, South Korea's volcanic crown jewel floating off the southern coast. If you've ever booked a flight and then stared blankly at a map wondering where to even begin, you're not alone. The entire island is essentially one giant attraction, and while information is everywhere, sorting through it to find the genuinely worthwhile spots — the ones with efficient routes and truly jaw-dropping views — is a real challenge.

This guide is built for travelers planning a three- or four-day trip who want to experience Jeju to the fullest without wasting a single hour. Whether you're traveling with family, as a couple, or with a group of friends, these ten essential destinations are organized by region, complete with photo spots, budget tips, and a ready-to-use itinerary. With hydrangeas in full bloom right now and beach season officially open, the timing couldn't be better.

I've stripped out the clutter and kept only the places that earn a confident "yes, absolutely go." Read to the end and you'll have everything you need to build your own perfect Jeju trip.

## What to Know Before Visiting Jeju

The best places to visit in Jeju are spread across four broad regions: east, west, south, and north. The east is home to natural wonders like Seongsan Ilchulbong, Udo Island, and the Bijarim forest. The south is anchored by Seogwipo, with its waterfalls and dramatic columnar cliffs. The west offers the famous emerald beaches of Hyeopjae and Geumneung, plus the trendy seaside cafés of Aewol. The north revolves around Jeju City and the airport.

Because these regions are so far-flung, **renting a car is practically essential**. Public transportation will dramatically limit how much you can see in a single day, leaving the most beautiful corners of the island frustratingly out of reach.

Each season has its own signature charm. Spring brings golden canola fields, early summer (right now) means hydrangeas at their peak, autumn fills the hills with silver pampas grass, and winter blankets Hallasan in snow. As of late June, the island's hydrangea spots and beaches are at their absolute best.

One word of warning: peak summer means crowded parking lots and a fierce battle for reservations. Popular cafés, ferries to Udo Island, and certain experience-based attractions can fill up fast, so confirming bookings ahead of time is the safer move. Checking parking availability and operating hours before you set out will make your daily route far smoother.

## #1 — Seongsan Ilchulbong (A UNESCO World Heritage Site)

Rising dramatically from the sea on Jeju's eastern edge, Seongsan Ilchulbong is a UNESCO-listed volcanic crater that looks like an enormous crown bursting out of the ocean. The name translates to "Sunrise Peak," and while it's famous for spectacular sunrises, the view is every bit as breathtaking in broad daylight.

The hike to the summit follows a fairly steep stairway and takes about 25 minutes on foot. It's not a strenuous climb, but there's little shade along the way, so during summer months you'll want a hat, plenty of water, and an early-morning or late-afternoon visit. There's a small admission fee for adults, though it's always worth checking the official site for current pricing and hours before you go.

On your way down, don't miss the haenyeo (female free-diver) performance at Udumeolgae Beach below the peak. These legendary women have harvested seafood by hand for generations, and watching them up close offers a rare glimpse into Jeju's living culture. It's an easy choice for the number one spot among the best places to visit in Jeju.

## #2 — Udo Island (The Island Shaped Like a Lying Cow)

Named for its resemblance to a cow lying down, Udo is a tiny island just a 15-minute ferry ride from Seongsan Port. Ferries typically run at regular intervals from morning to evening, but schedules shift with the season and weather, so always confirm the day's sailings. During peak season, bringing your own car may be restricted, which is why rented electric carts and bicycle tours around the island are so popular.

Udo's biggest draw is, without question, the color of its water. The black sand of Geommeolle Beach contrasts vividly with the emerald sea, while Hagosudong Beach is famous for its white sand and crystal-clear shallows — a guaranteed photo spot. Follow the coastal road that loops the island and you'll find a postcard-worthy frame at every turn.

Be sure to try the island's signature peanut ice cream, made from Udo-grown peanuts. A half-day here is plenty, making it one of the most rewarding island escapes among all the places worth visiting in Jeju.

## #3–5 — Natural Wonders (Hallasan, Cheonjiyeon Falls, and the Columnar Joint Cliffs)

Coming in at number three is the heart of the island itself: **Hallasan**, South Korea's highest mountain. For beginners, the Yeongsil or Eorimok trails are the way to go. Yeongsil is relatively short and rewards you with striking rock formations and wide-open vistas, while Eorimok follows a gentler ridge that's manageable even for families. The trail to the Baengnokdam summit crater may require an advance reservation, so check ahead depending on your plans.

Number four, **Cheonjiyeon Falls**, sits close to downtown Seogwipo, making it wonderfully accessible. A walk through lush subtropical forest leads you to a cascade thundering down into a clear pool. During evening hours, illuminated lighting reportedly transforms the scene into something genuinely dreamlike.

Number five, the **Daepo Jusangjeolli columnar cliffs**, formed as volcanic lava cooled into perfect hexagonal stone pillars along the coast. With waves crashing against the cliffs and well-designed viewing platforms, it's an ideal place to capture photos against a backdrop that nature carved by hand.

## #6–8 — Atmospheric Gems (Volcanic Hills, Cafés, and Ocean Views)

Jeju's true soul lives in its oreum — the small volcanic cones scattered across the island. Number six, **Saebyeol Oreum**, is beloved for its gentle slopes, waving pampas grass, and panoramic 360-degree views from the top. **Abu Oreum** offers something different: a ring of cedar trees encircling its crater, creating a uniquely satisfying composition when viewed from above. Best of all, both are completely free to enter, making them unbeatable value.

Number seven brings us to **Hyeopjae and Geumneung Beaches**. With tiny Biyangdo Island on the horizon and emerald water lapping against white sand, this is the defining image of Jeju's west coast — equally beloved as a swimming spot and a photographer's dream.

Number eight, the **seaside café strip in Aewol**, is especially popular with couples and groups of friends. A row of cafés with floor-to-ceiling windows opens onto the ocean, and visiting around sunset is when the atmosphere truly peaks. If you're hunting for the most aesthetically pleasing places to visit in Jeju, make sure this region earns a spot on your itinerary.

## #9–10 — Family and Kid-Friendly Picks (Theme Parks and Hands-On Fun)

Traveling with children? Number nine pairs **Bijarim Forest** with **Ecoland**. Bijarim is a woodland of nutmeg trees that are hundreds of years old, with flat, stroller-friendly paths winding through the canopy. Simply breathing in the phytoncide-rich air as you stroll is deeply restorative. Ecoland, meanwhile, is a theme park where you ride a train through a primeval Gotjawal forest — a guaranteed hit with kids.

Number ten is your reliable backup plan for rainy days. **Aqua Planet Jeju** is one of Korea's largest aquariums, letting visitors get up close with a remarkable variety of marine life. Beyond that, museums and indoor theme parks are scattered across every region, acting as insurance against Jeju's notoriously unpredictable weather.

If you're seeking family-friendly places to visit in Jeju, the smart move is to pair each outdoor attraction with an indoor alternative ahead of time.

## Local Eats to Pair With Your Sightseeing

It's no exaggeration to say half the joy of a Jeju trip is the food. The undisputed star is grilled black pork (heukdwaeji), prized for its rich flavor and chewy texture. Dedicated black pork streets exist throughout Jeju City and Seogwipo, so you'll find one easily near your accommodation. For a heartier meal, try gogi-guksu — thick slices of pork served over noodles in a deep, milky bone broth that's distinctly Jeju.

If you're exploring the southern Seogwipo region, seek out braised or grilled hairtail fish (galchi). A whole silvery hairtail braised into a single dish is a luxury you can only fully savor here. A stop at a traditional market like Dongmun Market also opens up fresh sashimi, sweet shrimp, and cuttlefish at reasonable prices.

The key to smart routing is bundling attractions with restaurants. Visit Seongsan Ilchulbong in the east, then grab lunch at a nearby haenyeo-run eatery; enjoy Hyeopjae Beach in the west, then settle into an Aewol café for dessert. Linking your stops this way saves a tremendous amount of travel time.

## A No-Wasted-Time 3-Day Itinerary

An efficient route through the best places to visit in Jeju starts with clustering attractions by region. From the airport, follow a broad east → south → west flow so your paths never overlap.

**Day 1 (East):** After landing, pick up your rental car and head east. Stroll the Bijarim forest trail, hike Seongsan Ilchulbong, then take a half-day tour of Udo Island. Spend the night near Seongsan and enjoy dinner at a haenyeo restaurant.

**Day 2 (South):** Visit Cheonjiyeon Falls and the Daepo columnar cliffs, then grab braised hairtail for lunch in Seogwipo. In the afternoon, tackle the Hallasan Yeongsil trail, or wind down at a café if you're feeling the heat.

**Day 3 (West):** Take a morning walk up Saebyeol Oreum, splash around at Hyeopjae or Geumneung Beach, then watch the sunset from an Aewol seaside café before heading back to the airport.

This is a realistic pace of three to four stops per day that accounts for travel time. If you have four days, add a half-day of breathing room to each region to dive deeper into the cafés and restaurants.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How many days do I need to see all the best places to visit in Jeju?**
A: Since the top ten are spread evenly across all four regions, three days is realistic for hitting one or two key spots per region in a tight loop — the east → south → west route above is a great template. With four days, you can fold in cafés, restaurants, and extra oreum for a more relaxed pace. Cramming in five or more stops a day will leave you too exhausted to even take good photos, so stick to three or four.

**Q: Can I get around Jeju without a rental car?**
A: It's possible, but far less efficient. City buses and tourist loop buses exist, but they run infrequently, and outlying oreum and beaches are hard to reach. If you can drive, a rental car is by far the most convenient option; if not, taxi tours or driver-accompanied package tours are solid alternatives. They cost more but plan your route for you and spare your legs.

**Q: When is the best time of year to visit Jeju?**
A: Each season has its own appeal. Spring brings canola flowers, early summer (like right now) offers hydrangeas and beaches, autumn lights up Saebyeol Oreum with pampas grass, and winter delivers snowy Hallasan vistas. Just keep in mind that summer peak season and holidays bring the biggest crowds and highest lodging prices — if you want peace and quiet, target weekdays or the off-season.

**Q: Are any of the best places to visit in Jeju free to enter?**
A: Plenty are. Hyeopjae and Geumneung Beaches, oreum like Saebyeol and Abu, and certain columnar cliff viewpoints are all free. Meanwhile, Seongsan Ilchulbong, Cheonjiyeon Falls, and Aqua Planet charge admission, generally ranging from small to moderate for adults. Mixing in the free spots is a great way to build a budget-friendly yet fulfilling itinerary.

## Final Thoughts

We've now walked through the ten best places to visit in Jeju, from Seongsan Ilchulbong and Udo Island to Hallasan, Hyeopjae Beach, and Saebyeol Oreum, all organized by region. The takeaway is simple: cluster your route by area, pair every outdoor attraction with an indoor backup, and connect nearby restaurants and cafés to your sightseeing stops. Follow just those three principles and you'll have a rich, efficient trip with no wasted time.
