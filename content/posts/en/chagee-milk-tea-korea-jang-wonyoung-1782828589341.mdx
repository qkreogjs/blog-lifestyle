## What Is Chagee — the Milk Tea Behind Wonyoung's "So Good!"?

Chagee (CHAGEE) is a global premium milk tea brand that started in China and expanded rapidly across Southeast Asia — Malaysia, Singapore, Thailand, and beyond. What sets it apart from the powder-based milk teas you usually find is its commitment to brewing tea from actual whole tea leaves (what the brand calls "original leaf" tea). That foundation gives the drinks a cleaner, more aromatic profile, leaning on the natural fragrance and crisp finish of real tea rather than a heavy, artificial sweetness. On top of that, Chagee is known for its sleek, design-forward cups and minimalist store interiors that reinterpret traditional tea culture in a modern, Instagram-friendly way.

The single biggest reason Chagee blew up in Korea, though, is Wonyoung. ## The 5 Best Chagee Milk Tea Menu Items You Have to Try

Walking into Chagee for the first time can be a little overwhelming — there are a lot of options. To make it easier, here's a rundown organized by taste preference. Keep in mind that the lineup can vary by store and country, so consider this a guide to the generally popular picks.

1. **Signature Milk Tea (Boya Jadeite line)**: This is Chagee's flagship, and the one most people associate with the brand. A deeply brewed tea base meets smooth milk, giving you something far cleaner and more aromatic than your average powdered milk tea. If it's your first visit, this is the one to order, no question.
2. **Jasmine-Based Milk Tea**: A delicate, floral option that isn't too sweet. It's bright and refreshing — perfect if you want the natural fragrance of the tea to shine through.
3. **Oolong-Based Milk Tea**: If you like a richer, more toasty, full-bodied flavor, go for this one. It balances beautifully with the milk and tends to be a crowd-pleaser across all ages.
4. **Fruit Tea Line**: On days when a creamy milk tea feels like too much, a zesty fruit-based tea is a great alternative — and a lighter choice if you're watching the calories.
5. **Seasonal Limited Editions**: Chagee leans heavily into seasonal drops, so there's often a limited-time drink on the menu. Always scan for the seasonal specials when you visit.

**How to choose by taste**: For your first time, order it at **50% sweetness with regular ice** so you can actually taste the tea itself. If you have a sweet tooth, bump up the sugar level; if you want a softer, chewier texture, add toppings — that's usually where people get the most satisfaction.
