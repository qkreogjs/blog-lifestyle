## What Exactly Is CHAGEE? — The Tea Brand That Became a Global Phenomenon

CHAGEE was founded in 2017 in Yunnan, China, as a tea-focused beverage brand. It started small, but it scaled fast — expanding across Southeast Asia (Malaysia, Singapore, Thailand, and more) before pushing into the United States. In just a handful of years it grew its store count dramatically, earning a reputation as something of a "milk tea unicorn" with a textbook rapid-growth story.

So what sets CHAGEE apart from the bubble tea you already know? The biggest difference is its **fresh-brewed loose-leaf tea base**. Instead of the thick, ultra-sweet syrup-forward milk teas most of us picture, CHAGEE aims to highlight the actual aroma and flavor of brewed tea leaves. That's why it earns especially high marks from people who genuinely want to *taste the tea* — not just sugar and creamer.

Its positioning is unusual, too. CHAGEE doesn't present itself as a cheap-and-cheerful bubble tea shop. Instead, it positions itself as a **premium tea brand gunning for Starbucks** — clean, modern store interiors, consistent brand design, and marketing that leans hard on tea quality. Thanks to that differentiation, CHAGEE has become a daily-drink staple for young consumers across Southeast Asia and the U.S. — and it's that same premium identity that **Chagee Korea** is expected to bring with it.

## 5 CHAGEE Signature Menu Items You Have to Try

Walking into a CHAGEE store for the first time can be overwhelming, so here's a category-by-category cheat sheet built around the brand's headline drinks. (Menu names and recipes are based on what's known from overseas stores, and some details may change once the Korean menu is finalized.)

**1. Boya Jasmine (伯牙绝弦)** — This is CHAGEE's signature and best-seller, and the drink that comes up most often. It's a jasmine loose-leaf tea base blended with milk, with the tea aroma kept gently intact, which is why it's loved by everyone from first-timers to die-hard fans. If you've never had CHAGEE before, this is *the* one to start with.

**2. Original Loose-Leaf Tea** — For anyone who wants to enjoy pure, unadulterated tea flavor without milk. It's not sweet and goes down clean, making it a great after-meal drink.

**3. Fruit Tea Series** — A refreshing blend of bright fruit and tea, ideal served iced on a hot day. This is your go-to when you want something light and thirst-quenching.

**4 & 5. Seasonal Specials & Milk-Forward Teas** — If you love a richer cup, go for the milk-heavy line; if you prefer something lighter, choose a tea-forward option. CHAGEE's range lets you dial the experience up or down.

**Customization tip:** If you're new, start with **50% sweetness and regular ice** so you can taste the tea aroma first. If you lean toward bolder flavors, go **70–100% sweetness with less ice** to push the milk-and-tea concentration higher. CHAGEE drinks change character dramatically depending on sweetness level, so for your first visit, err on the less-sweet side and adjust from there.
