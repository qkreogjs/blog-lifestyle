## What Is CHAGEE? The Brand Behind the Global Tea Phenomenon

CHAGEE was founded in 2017 in Yunnan, China — a region famous for its tea culture. It started small, but it scaled fast. The brand expanded across Southeast Asia (Malaysia, Singapore, Thailand, and beyond), pushed into the U.S. market, and grew its store count at a pace that earned it a reputation as one of the fastest-rising tea chains in the world. If you've heard people describe it as a "tea empire," that's not marketing fluff — the growth has genuinely been remarkable.

So what sets CHAGEE apart from the bubble tea shop on every corner? The biggest difference is its **fresh, brewed-leaf tea base**. Instead of the heavy, syrup-forward sweetness you get from a lot of mass-market bubble tea, CHAGEE leans into the actual aroma and flavor of real brewed tea leaves. That's why it tends to win over people who genuinely enjoy tea — the leaf character comes through rather than getting buried under sugar.

The brand's positioning is unusual too. CHAGEE doesn't present itself as a cheap, grab-and-go boba spot. It positions itself as a **premium tea brand aiming squarely at Starbucks** — clean, modern store designs, consistent branding, and marketing that emphasizes tea quality over gimmicks. Think of it as building a "tea instead of coffee" café culture rather than just selling sugary drinks. That differentiation is exactly why CHAGEE became a daily ritual for younger customers across Southeast Asia and the U.S., not just an occasional treat.

## 5 CHAGEE Signature Drinks You Have to Try

Walking up to the menu for the first time can feel overwhelming, so here's a category-by-category breakdown of what to order. (Menu names and recipes are based on what's known from existing international locations and may vary slightly by market.)

**1. Boya Jasmine (伯牙绝弦)** — This is the signature and the bestseller, hands down the most-mentioned CHAGEE drink anywhere. It's a jasmine leaf-tea base blended with milk, and the tea aroma stays beautifully present. It's the perfect entry point for newcomers but refined enough that longtime fans keep coming back. If it's your first time, this is *the* one to start with.

**2. Pure Leaf Tea (no milk)** — For anyone who wants the tea flavor unadulterated. It's clean, not sweet, and makes a great after-meal drink when you don't want anything heavy.

**3. Fruit Tea Series** — Bright, refreshing blends of fruit and tea. Served iced on a hot day, it's the kind of drink that disappears in about ten minutes.

**4 & 5. Seasonal Specials and Milk-Forward Blends** — If you love a richer, creamier cup, go for the milk-heavy lineup. If you prefer something lighter, choose a drink where the tea ratio dominates. The seasonal specials are also worth watching for limited-time flavors.

**Customization tip:** If you're new, start with **50% sweetness and regular ice** so you can actually taste the tea aroma first. If you already know you like it bold, bump up to **70–100% sweetness with less ice** to intensify the milk-and-tea richness. CHAGEE drinks change character a lot depending on the sweetness level, so going easy on the sugar for your first visit is the smart play.
